Gradient Descent is an iterative method to find the minimum of a function by moving in the opposite direction of the gradient.

The learning rate \(\alpha\) controls how big each step is. Choosing \(\alpha\) is crucial:

  • Too large: May overshoot the minimum.
  • Too small: Convergence becomes very slow.

In higher dimensions, the concept remains the same, but we deal with gradient vectors.
